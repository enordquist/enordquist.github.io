--- title: "Protein-like nanopore dewetting" excerpt: "Why do protein ion channels like the Bk channel, which are quite large ~1.2 nm diameter, have vapor rather than physical barriers?
" collection: portfolio ---

In nano-scale confined environments, water exists in a delicate equilibrium between condensed and vapor phase. We are using simple model nanopores to study the effects of pore morphology and co-solvent concentration on the free energy cost of dewetting, in order to apply what I learn to real proteins like the BK channel. This work is being done with Sam Schultz, currently an undergrad at UMass.
